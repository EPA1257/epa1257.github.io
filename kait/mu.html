 with contributions from: Grayson Scaife, Hayden Dale, Trinnidy Smith-Purkis, Sarah Dilloo, Sebastian Garcia

 After we decided the Govoice name was worn out, this project (another one in the works for quite a time, originally under the stolen name of Parlophon Orchestr) was birthed, also from a desire to take the extended jam sessions to the next level. The one and only recording, "Heir of Dreams", contains every single note we ever recorded, then painstakingly mixed into an extremely incoherent mess over the span of three or four sleepless nights by yours truly. After it was released, a lot of things changed.

Lozenge/Cats Chase Mice

Two very different projects, both with my friend Guy Kesnick. Cats Chase Mice came first and was a sort of split between us two in the traditional sense, in that each one of us did a track separately, so both were done in our "traditional" styles for the time. We recorded most of CCM in 2017 but it was only released in 2018 after we finished working on the first record for Lozenge called "Mixed Barries". Again a split, but we tried a lot of new techniques (for example, I started incorporating field recordings, and Guy began using more samples, which are now a staple of his work). Lozenge was originally intended to be an experimental noise project but has since morphed into a weird little corner of music and not-music, with the constantly-changing upcoming album "Centres" being the literal centre of that philosophy. The first single "Fuck Off FedEx/Terrain" was released in December 2021 and two vinyl exclusive maxi-singles have since followed, with the album still being meticulously worked on by the both of us at our own respective paces.

Govoice

A band born out of various jam sessions at our local library's music studio, with a desire to create "pure fuckin' noise" as I called it at the time, we were pretty decently prolific and released 4 full-length albums in the span of 2 months! Through a constantly-changing lineup (with some albums not featuring some members, making it sort of a communal thing) we were able to churn out some intriguing sounds throughout the rest of the late 2010s. The infamously delayed and complicated "Cleanup in Aisle 7" album was completely discarded and then remade with an entirely different sound. The album "Ichthyolatry", originally intended as a grindcore record, became a weird little improvised folk jam session, and was popular between our family members and circle of friends. We switched up the formula a bit for "Angry Birds Blanket House" and also released the in-between failures and outtakes as a companion record called "Glaciers of Gabon". Another EP and a greatest-hits compilation did well, but we thought the sound was drying up, so the now-finished "Cleanup in Aisle 7" and another set of new recordings called "Get Well Soon" were released as the final Govoice album. We made about $5 from it all. What a weird little band.

Imagination

The first real band I ever did, with my friend Grayson. Known for our seemingly-still-popular song "It's a Vortex". We were always acapella or on piano, then when we did the second album we started experimenting a lot in a bunch of weird ways, with the janky techno, sound collages of YouTube Poop videos, and some piano improv. We looked back on it as "cringy" later, so our next couple releases took the piss of that. In 2018 we released "Theory Day" which gets beyond the strangeness and propels directly into hellish territory. There are two further tracks on the "FUTURE" compilation released in 2019, extended bass and keyboard jams.

Listen to the most popular thing we did, our first album, titled "The World", here

 with Matt Dargen - Forty Years in the Forest

Dargen is a multimedia artist from the USA who has made a lot of interesting work in the fields of noise, ambient, grooves, and surrealist artworks in the form of webpages, comics, collages, etc.

He sent me a few hours of stuff, which has been edited down significantly here, and I sent scans of an old book I had for art inspiration. I was very pleased with the result and I hope he was too. 

 

with BetaRiffs - Dirt Angel

BetaRiffs is the primary alias of my friend Guy Kesnick (see also: "You will hate this"). This is a very stupid collaboration recorded in one night through a Discord call. Guy made the last track on his own. It's interesting but this one was a joke mostly, so you can skip it if you like.

 

with Lame Sauce - Flavorwave

One of many aliases of Max Burchell, a very talented graphic designer, video editor and musician from Nebraska. Their work under this alias varies from chiptune remixes to synth jams to French House experiments. For this EP, we sampled Guy Fieri. I experimented in GarageBand a lot (as I was doing on other releases of mine at the time), and Max went for a chill electro beats style. It is the perfect clash and I'm very glad it exists.
