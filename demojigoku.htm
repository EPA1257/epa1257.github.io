Copy Girl. Recorded August 2025.
* This will not be on the album.
This is a cover of a song by COCA, whose real name is Yoshiko Hada. Today she is well known as a painter, but not many people know she was a musician who was involved with the Japanese indie scene until 1999. She was an original member of indie trio ELEKKI BRAN which is how I found out about her.
In the 90s she released some music under this pseudonym and then quietly disappeared. I don't know if they really made a dent in the scene at the time, I can't find any articles or anything, but the songs are incredible. Offbeat pop? nah that feels like a misnomer. It's something. Something crazy about these songs, and her only full length album PULSE.
I have cried to Copy Girl many times. I dont think Im too ashamed to admit that now, but if i said it at the time, I feel like I would have been laughed at, probably not. Really its a simple song but those lyrics cryptic as they are, describe a part of me i like to pretend to repress. It is a song I live through every day of my life.
I wanted to learn to play this song, but in my mind this song wouldnt fit well with the theme of this album. But I wanted to pay tribute to what is a very important song for me, and one that sparked the creation of my new world of songwriting. Thanks Hada-san.
Japanese is still a language I struggle with. I can now understand it better than French. A blessing and a curse. My pronunciation is not good, very Canadian. Please keep that in mind. The lyrics are on Genius if you want to read/translate them.

"Someone" vocal demo & songwriting session. Recorded July 2025.
This is the vocal demo of a song called "someone" - the actual title of the song is longer, ok? - a cool little short thing that originally ended before the 'Savagely' line. listen to the medley to get an idea of the instrumental structure as it stands right now. when I tried to record bass for this song i found out my bass is broken, how fun, I guess I need to fix that!!!
0:00 - 1:15 - I'm figuring out the melody
1:16 - 2:22 - the songwriting process in action, minus a LOT of silence. You are hearing the ending being written, the words being formed from my mouth for the first time. The tapping is me writing it down in my notes app. Exciting.
2:23 - end - Pretty much the final melody/lyrics (except for a few changes).
After this, I figured out the chords (thanks Guy for helping with that!).
